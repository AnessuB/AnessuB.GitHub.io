Bull burger

  • Time: 6mins
  • Serves: One
Ingredients Quantity
Potato bun 1
Angus beef 150gm
Cheese slice 1
Bacon Jam 15gm
Red Onion 3 rings
Bull sauce 30gm

Step 1: Roll bun on butter and toast to a nice crispy golden brown on griddle at 130.

Step 2: Hand smash 150gm beef patty on griddle and cook for 2minutes before flipping.

Step 3: Place one slice cheese on the patty and let it cook for 1minute 30seconds and top with 15gm Bacon jam.

Step 4: Take the patty and place it on bottom bun, place 3 rings red onion

Step 5: Apply bull sauce on the top bun and place firmly on top and burger is ready to serve.

Bucatini All'Amatriciana

  • Time: 29mins
  • Serves: Two
Ingredients Quantity
bucatini pasta 5 ounces
extra-virgin olive oil ¼ cup
crushed garlic 3 cloves
guanciale (cured pork cheek), sliced 1 ½ ounces
sliced red onion ¼ cup
red pepper flakes 1 pinch
can crushed San Marzano tomatoes ½ (8 ounce)
salt and ground black pepper to taste
freshly grated Pecorino Romano cheese 1 ounce

Step 1:Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Stir in bucatini and return to a boil. Cook, uncovered, stirring occasionally, until bucatini is tender, about 11 minutes. Drain.

Step 2:Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic cloves; cook until golden brown, about 1 minute. Remove with a slotted spoon and discard. Add guanciale; cook and stir until crisp and golden, about 4 minutes. Add onion and red pepper flakes; cook and stir until onion is translucent, about 3 minutes. Stir in tomatoes, salt, and black pepper. Simmer tomato sauce until flavors combine, about 10 minutes.

Step 3:Stir bucatini and Pecorino Romano cheese into tomato sauce and toss until evenly coated.

Sweet and Spicy Turkey Sandwich

Description

Pepperjack cheese and strawberry preserves mingle on this turkey sandwich for a sweet and savory lunch. Reviewer Jillian says, "I would have never thought to pair turkey, pepperjack cheese, and strawberry preserves but this definitely works and is a nice departure from your 'run of the mill' turkey sandwich.

  • Time: 10mins
  • Serves: One
Ingredients Quantity
(1/2 inch thick) hearty country bread 2 slices
roasted turkey breast 4 slices
pepperjack cheese 1 slice
butter 2 teaspoons
strawberry preserves 4 teaspoons

Step 1:Heat a small skillet over medium heat. Butter one side of each of the bread slices with one teaspoon butter. Place one slice, butter side down, in the skillet. Top with the turkey and cheese slices. Place the second slice of bread on top, butter side up.

Step 2:When the first side of the sandwich is golden brown, turn and brown the other side, 3 to 5 minutes per side, or until the cheese begins to melt.

Step 3:Remove sandwich to a plate and top with strawberry preserves, or serve the preserves on the side.
